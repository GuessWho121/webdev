Why Donate Blood?

"A single pint can save three lives, a single gesture can create a million smiles."

Blood donation is a life-saving act that helps millions of patients suffering from severe illnesses, accidents, surgeries, and medical conditions like anemia and blood disorders. Despite the increasing demand, India still faces a shortage of blood donors.

Key Statistics About Blood Donation in India

  • Daily Blood Requirement: India requires around 38,000 blood donations daily.
  • Annual Blood Demand: The country needs about 14.6 million units of blood every year.
  • Shortfall: India faces a shortage of 1.9-2.1 million blood units annually.
  • Voluntary Donors: Only about 1% of India’s population donates blood regularly.
  • Blood Shelf Life: Donated whole blood can be stored for only 35-42 days.
  • Universal Donors: Less than 7% of India’s population has O-negative blood.
  • Thalassemia Patients: India has over 100,000 thalassemia patients relying on regular transfusions.

Who Needs Blood?

  • Accident victims: Over 500,000 road accident victims in India require urgent blood transfusions yearly.
  • Pregnant women: Around 70,000 maternal deaths annually are linked to severe blood loss.
  • Cancer patients: Patients undergoing chemotherapy often require multiple blood transfusions.
  • Surgical patients: Blood is critical for surgeries, organ transplants, and other medical procedures.

Benefits of Donating Blood

  • Saves Lives: One donation can save up to 3 lives.
  • Health Benefits: Regular donation helps maintain heart health.
  • Free Health Screening: Donors receive a mini health check-up.
  • Community Contribution: Donating blood fosters a sense of community.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Age: 18-65 years
  • Weight: Minimum 45 kg
  • General Good Health: No active infections or chronic illnesses.
  • Hemoglobin: Not less than 12.5 g/dL

The Donation Process

  • Registration: Donor provides personal details and consent.
  • Health Check-Up: Assessment of medical history and basic health parameters.
  • Donation: The actual blood collection process, typically lasting 10-15 minutes.
  • Post-Donation Care: Rest and refreshments to help the donor recover.
